Bulgarian cities - Pomorie


POMORIE WEATHER

Pomorie is a town in southeastern Bulgaria. It is located in Burgas region. The town is the administrative center of Pomorie municipality.


In Pomorie you can find many sights such as


Salt Museum

The Salt Museum in Pomorie is the only one of its kind in Eastern Europe that produces salt by solar evaporation of sea water. It was opened in 2002 and is located on the shores of Lake Pomorie.

Ancient domed tomb

The ancient domed tomb of Pomorie is located west of the town of Pomorie. The tomb dates from the II-III century AD. It is believed that it served as a heroine (mausoleum) of a rich Anhial family, in which religious pagan rituals were performed.

Boyar Wine Cellar

In 1998 the brothers Simeon and Dimitar Boychevi together with Argir Hristoforov created 350 decares of vineyards and built a wine cellar "Boyar". The classic underground cellar with French and California oak barrels, the fully automated bottling line.

Municipal Museum and Gallery

Historical Museum - Pomorie has been preserving and exhibiting the material part of the cultural and historical heritage of the town and the region for almost two decades. The museum building is a cultural monument. It was built during the last two decades of the 19th century, which originally housed a Greek girls' school.

Library and cinema "Prosveta-1888"

The library at Chitalishte "Prosveta - 1888" is the largest in the municipality of Pomorie and is housed in a new, modern building with very good conditions. It has a rich book collection, a modernly equipped reading room, a children's department and an adult department. A cinema with 350 seats has been built.

Architectural reserve "Old Pomorie houses"

The reserve "Old Pomorie houses" is located near the sea. The buildings in the reserve were built in the XIX century. The typical Pomorie house, which tourists can see while walking through the cobbled streets, has two floors - a stone ground floor and a rafter floor.
