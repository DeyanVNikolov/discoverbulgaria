Bulgarian cities - Blagoevgrad


BLAGOEVGRAD WEATHER

Blagoevgrad is a city in Bulgaria. It is the economic, cultural and educational center of Southwestern Bulgaria and the administrative center of the district and municipality of the same name.


In Blagoevgrad you can find many sights such as


Clocktower

The Blagoevgrad Clock Tower is a Revival building, one of the symbols of Blagoevgrad and one of the oldest clock towers in Bulgaria. It was built in 1867 in Gorna Dzhumaya by Maleshev masters. There is a unique Swiss watch mechanism, also made in the XIX century. It is a rectangular truncated pyramid with a pointed roof with copper cladding.

Monument to Gotse Delchev

The monument is the work of sculptor Krum Dermendzhiev and was unveiled on August 2, 1955, after the announcement of a national competition by the Ministry of Culture. The height of the monument together with the pedestal is 8 meters. The sculpture is made of bronze and the pedestal is made of granite. To this day it remains one of the symbols of Blagoevgrad.

Regional historical museum

If you are in Blagoevgrad, you will be impressed by the original building of the Regional Historical Museum - Blagoevgrad, neatly located in the cozy neighborhood of Varosha. Built in 1978, designed by architect Ivan Bitrakov, this is one of the few buildings in Bulgaria built specifically for a museum (all others are simply adapted for this purpose).

Ethnographic complex Varosha

Varosha is an ancient neighborhood in Blagoevgrad with narrow cobbled streets and authentic architecture from the late seventeenth and eighteenth centuries. It is located on the east bank of the river Blagoevgradska Bistritsa. In the 18th century it was the only neighborhood with a Christian population in the city. During the Renaissance, the best teachers and prominent cultural figures from the region lived there, who took an active part in the revolutionary struggles of the Bulgarians.

Volga Park

Volga Park is located in the central part of Blagoevgrad, close to Macedonia Square. Its name comes from the Volga Hotel, which operated until years ago (it now houses the National Revenue Agency). A small and very nice park, where one can find the necessary coolness during the summer heat. Around it are - the building of the National Revenue Agency, as well as the hotel "Monte Cristo" (Old Mineral Bath).

House-museum of Georgi Izmirliev The Macedonian

Georgi Dimitrov Izmirliev, known as the Macedonian, was a Bulgarian revolutionary and public figure, a participant in the April Uprising of 1876 as an assistant to the Apostle Stefan Stambolov and a military leader in the First (Tarnovo) Revolutionary District. 100 years after his death, in 1976, his native house in Blagoevgrad was declared a museum.
