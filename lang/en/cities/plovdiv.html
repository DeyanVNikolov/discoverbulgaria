Bulgarian cities - Plovdiv


PLOVDIV WEATHER

The city is the most dynamically developing center in Southern Bulgaria. Its economy is growing of the well-developed diversified industry, of services, tourism and information technologies. Many cultural events are held in Plovdiv: the international fair, the international theater festival "Scene at the Crossroads", "Kapana Fest", "Night of Plovdiv", the music forum "Sounds of The Ages", the international folklore festival "Plovdiv" and others.


In Plovdiv you can find many sights such as


Rowing base

The rowing base in Plovdiv is a sports facility designed for exercising rowing sport. It is located in the Recreation and Culture Park, next to the Plovdiv Stadium and the Plovdiv Stadium zoo. This is the largest such base in the Balkans, where rowing competitions can be held sports.

"The main street"

The main street, often abbreviated Main, is the main pedestrian zone in the city center Plovdiv. It houses theaters, galleries, shops, cafes and some of the city's landmarks. Often the Main itself is defined as a landmark. The main one stretches from Central Square to Novotel "Plovdiv" has a total length of 1.8 km, which makes it the longest pedestrian street in Europe.

"Nebet Tepe"

Nebet Tepe is one of today's six hills, preserved to this day, around which it is built the city of Plovdiv. Together with Jambaz Tepe and Taksim Tepe it forms the Three Hills, today better known as Ancient Plovdiv, where the ancient city was located. The archeological complex "Nebet Tepe" is recognized as a cultural monument of national importance. Remains of the first one have been discovered in it a prehistoric settlement dating back to the Stone-Copper Age

Ancient theater

It was built during the time of the Roman emperor Mark Ulpius Trajan (98 - 117) AD. and is discovered during archeological excavations from 1968 to 1979 by the Archaeological Museum of Plovdiv. It is located between Jambaz Tepe and Taksim Tepe. The Philippopolis Theater is the only preserved ancient theater building in the Bulgarian lands. The building is adapted for the modern cultural life of Plovdiv and takes place in it different in nature performances for about 3500 spectators. It has been declared an archeological monument in cash. 6 on State Gazette since 1995

The singing fountains

one music attraction was opened after renovation on May 22, 2015. The famous "Singers fountains "in the city park of Plovdiv are located on 6 decares, and their jets are fired up to 20 meters in height. The melodies that sound from the water facility are mostly classical.
During the summer season the Singing Fountains in Tsar Simeon's Garden start their music program from 9.30 pm o'clock. In the summer the day is bigger for this reason and the start of the light show is shifted to Plovdiv residents and guests of the city can enjoy the show. The schedule, as before, is in the days Thursday, Friday and Saturday and includes 5 music compositions that alternate each week. Their duration is up to 25 minutes. In winter, the attraction is preserved, but only with colorful artistic lighting.

The Monument of the Union

Saedinenie Square in Plovdiv is dedicated to the Saedinenie of Bulgaria and was built in connection with the 100th anniversary of the event. It is located north of Sixth of September Boulevard, between The Archaeological Museum, the Military Staff and the Appellate Prosecutor's Office, a few blocks west of the Main the street to reach the pedestrian bridge over the Maritsa River. Next to the square there is an underpass under the boulevard "Sixth September ”, known by the same name.
The monument is 12 meters in size and includes a figure of a young woman holding a laurel wreath over her head, personifying the Unification of Bulgaria and two majestically spread wings of a bird symbolizing the aspiration to the unity and the urge of the Bulgarians for freedom of the two divided parts of the country - the Principality of Bulgaria and Eastern Rumelia between 1878 and 1885. The wreath is a symbol of victory.
