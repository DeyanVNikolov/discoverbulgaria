Bulgarian cities - Stara Zagora


STARA ZAGORA WEATHER

Stara Zagora is a city in Southern Bulgaria, one of the main economic centers in the country, as well as a major transport hub of Southern Bulgaria. The city is the sixth largest in the country with a population of 137,100 people.


In Stara Zagora you can find many sights such as


Ancient Forum of Augustus Trajan

The Forum of the Ancient Roman City of Augusta Trayana is an archeological site in the central part of the city of Stara Zagora. The site was discovered after construction works in the area of ​​the Judicial Chamber in the city. The ancient forum complex is one of the most monumental facilities in the Roman city of Augusta Trayana.

Geo Milev House-Museum

House-Museum "Geo Milev" in Stara Zagora was opened on September 27, 1953. They are here exhibited more than 3000 exhibits related to the life and work of the great Bulgarian poet Geo Milev. Visitors can also watch a documentary about the poet and his legacy in modern times audio-visual room.

The steam locomotive of the 1923 insurgents

The steam locomotive of the insurgents from 1923 is located and placed in front of the railway station of Stara Zagora. The locomotive is number 555 and is manufactured in Germany. He is known to have participated in The September Uprising. The locomotive captured the station in Stara Zagora by the insurgents.

Shahan's House

Shahanovi is a prominent Stara Zagora industrial family from the end of the 19th and the first half of 20th century, specializing in the production of spirits. The ancestor of the family is Georgi Shahanov - who arrived in the city with a targeted lemonade from Lozengrad.

House-museum "City life of the XIX century"

The exposition presents the life of the people of Stara Zagora after the Liberation until the end of the XIX century. A rich collection of objects belonging to wealthy Stara Zagora families is on display.

Buhchev's house

Buhcheva house is one of the first houses in European style. It was built in 1910 by Austrian architect in Art Nouveau style - with richly decorated facade, columns, frescoes, with ethereal metal decorative elements, tapestries and murals.
