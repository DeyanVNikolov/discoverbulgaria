Bulgarian cities - Silistra


SILISTRA WEATHER

Silistra is a port city on the Danube River in northeastern Bulgaria. It is the administrative and economic center of the eponymous municipality of Silistra and district of Silistra.


In Silistra you can find many sights such as


Danube Garden

The Danube Garden is the first public and Danube park on the territory of Bulgaria. The park was established about 100-150 years ago. It was first mentioned in the newspaper "Turkey" in 1870.

Roman tomb

The Roman tomb with frescoes is one of the most brilliant monuments of tomb architecture not only in Bulgaria but also in the Balkans in late antiquity. It was discovered by chance in 1942 on the southern outskirts of Silistra.

Ethnographic Museum

Ethnographic Museum - Silistra was opened in 1967 and is part of the structure of RIM-Silistra. It is located in a two-storey building, built in the middle of the 19th century. The exposition is located on an area of ​​300 square meters, representing the way of life, livelihood, material and spiritual culture of the population of Silistra.

Mejidi Fortress Tabia

The fortress "Medjidi Tabia" is one of the best preserved examples of the Ottoman fortification from the middle of the XIX century. Today this fort is the only structure left from the fortress Silistra. Its construction took place in the period 1840-1853 according to the plan of the Prussian officer Helmut von Moltke.

Archaeological Museum

It is located on an area of ​​400 sq.m. in a building, a cultural monument, from the early twentieth century and stores over 20,000 monuments from prehistory to the eighteenth century. The representative exhibition features monuments from prehistoric, ancient and medieval eras.

Art Gallery

The Art Gallery in Silistra is located in the city center. It is housed in a beautiful building (former pedagogical school), which dates from 1890 and is in the style of "Late Art Nouveau". The gallery exhibits over 1500 works of art (painting, graphics, sculpture, etc.) of the great Bulgarian artists.
