Bulgarian Cities - Ruse


RUSE WEATHER

The population of the city is 144,533 people (according to GRAO data as of September 15, 2021), which confirms it as the fifth largest city in Bulgaria. Located 309 km from the capital Sofia and 74 km from the Romanian capital Bucharest, the city is a potential strategic intermodal and logistics center of the country and the South-Eastern Europe, given its geographical location, its established role as an important industrial center, as well as the presence of the country's first road and railway bridge over the Danube - Danube Bridge. The river joins the city and to the Pan-European Transport Corridor 7, creating conditions for commercial and cruise river navigation.


In Ruse you can find many sights such as


Danube Bridge - Ruse

The Danube Bridge, also called the Bridge of Friendship, is a steel farm bridge over the Danube between the cities of Ruse and Giurgiu (per river kilometer 488.7). Its length is 2.80 km and passes at a height of 30 meters above the water. It is built on 2 levels - for trains and cars. The middle part (85 m long) can be raised and let large ships pass. The bridge is one of the two bridges over the Danube River on the border between Bulgaria and Romania, which is about 500 km long.

The Viennese house

One of the most beautiful houses in Ruse is the so-called "Vienna House", located at the end of Blvd. Danube. It was completed in 1900. In 1902 it was bought by Ivan Stoyanov - Solaria - a trader in salt and gas in Giurgiu and Ruse. He is an honorary member of the Revolutionary Committee in Bucharest, assisting for years materially the Bulgarian revolutionaries and emigrants, the detachments of Hadji Dimitar, Stefan Karadja and Hristo Botev, is an activist for church independence, member of the National Assembly from 1882-1884, donor, public figure, factor in the national liberation struggles.

Monument of freedom

The composition is pyramidal. The statue at the top is a figure of a woman holding a sword in his left hand, pointing in the right direction where the liberators came from. One of the two a bronze lion at the base breaks the slave chains with its mouth, and the other guards the Sword and the Shield of Freedom. On the pedestal has relief images of volunteer scenes. The text of the main inscription reads: "To the proponents and volunteers who took part in the Liberation of Bulgaria in 1876-1877. "In the back of 2 cannons are placed on the base.

Ruse Opera

At this place in the city of Ruse before the Opera there was a church hall. The building of the Opera is with similar architecture. Its grand opening on November 27, 1949 was marked by the first premiere of the opera "La Traviata" by Giuseppe Verdi. The conductor is the famous Konstantin Iliev. From its inception until today, the Ruse Opera is permanently associated with generations of remarkable Bulgarian artists, which form the idea of ​​the high artistic level of the theater and build its international authority. With its intense creative life, the Ruse Opera has always been attractive to stars from world Wide.

Monument to Grandma Tonka

Tonka Obretenova was born in 1812 in the village of Cherven, near Ruse. Her parents are Toncho Suppliers and Minka Toncheva. She was married to a famous tailor and merchant in Ruse Tiho Obretenov (1831). From they have five boys and two girls. He took part in the organized national liberation struggle. It turns out to be serious organizational, moral and financial assistance to the Ruse Private Revolutionary Committee. Fellow and trusted person of Vasil Levski. Her home is a safe haven and organizational center for revolutionary activists. Her children are also involved in the struggle.

Ecomuseum

The Ecomuseum presents the diversity of animal species and protected areas along the Danube, as well as the coexistence of man with nature. The museum is located on four floors, where you can see a rich collection of fossils, as well as dioramas representing different animal species in a real environment. Here is the largest freshwater aquarium in Bulgaria with fish from the Danube and the rivers flowing into it. Special attention in the exhibition is separated from prehistoric animals. A model of a hairy mammoth (Mammuthus primigenius) in real is presented dimensions. Also on display is the world's only fully preserved part of another species of mammoth - the lower jaw. from Mammuthus rumanus.
