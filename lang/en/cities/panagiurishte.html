Bulgarian cities - Panagyurishte


Panagyurishte WEATHER

The town of Panagyurishte is located in a mountainous area. It lies in Sashtinska Sredna Gora. To the north of it, near the resort of Panagyurishte colonies, is Mount Bratia (1519 m above sea level). The river Panagyurska Luda Yana flows through the town, which after the village of Popintsi merges with the river Strelchanska Luda Yana and thus the river Luda Yana is formed.


In Panagyurishte you can find many sights such as


Museum of History

The Historical Museum in Panagyurishte is located in the city center. It is based on of a museum collection, opened in May 1943 in the community center "Videlina". The Historical Museum has the following Departments: Panagyurishte Golden Treasure, Participation in the Liberation Wars, Panagyurishte XV - XIX century, April uprising of 1876, Archeology, Natural History Collection, Crafts.

Monument to Raina Princess

Raina Popgeorgieva Futekova-Dipcheva, known as Raina Knyaginya, is a Bulgarian teacher, sewed the main insurgent flag of the Panagyurishte Revolutionary District for the April Uprising. On the day of announcing the uprising, she waved it along with Benkowski.

Raina Knyaginya House-Museum

One of the most visited places in Panagyurishte is the house-museum of Raina Futekova - main a teacher at a girls 'school who is only 20 years old when she sews the rebels' main flag. 22 April 1876 - the day of its consecration, at the request of the citizens she led the solemn procession, mounted a chosen horse, girded with a sword and revolver.

The Medicinal House-Museum

Ivan Lekov's house was built after the Crimean War and was painted in 1873. Its owner, a wealthy merchant, was a revolutionary and a participant in the April Uprising. The house is visited by the Apostle of Freedom - Vasil Levski.

The Blue Hadjidimitrova House-Museum

In the Blue Hadjidimitrova House-Museum in Panagyurishte there is an exposition of objects, donated by Ivan Deyanov. The exhibits are arranged in different collections - pottery, trade and production of copper, goldsmithing, woodcarving, works of art and manuscripts.

Тутевата къща-музей

The April Uprising was announced in the Tuteva House on April 20, 1876. Ivan Tutev was a wealthy Panagyurishte citizen and participant in the revolutionary cause. His house is the last residence of Benkovski in Panagyurishte. After the arrival of the courier with the news that Koprivshtitsa had revolted, the leaders led by Benkovski and Volov decided to raise the April Uprising.
