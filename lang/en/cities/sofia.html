Bulgarian Cities - Sofia


СОФИЯ ВРЕМЕТО

Sofia is the capital of Bulgaria and its largest city. It is the 14th largest city in number population in the European Union, with a population of 1,241,675 people (2019)

Sofia is a major administrative, industrial, transport, cultural and university center of the country, where 1/6 of the industrial production of Bulgaria is concentrated. They are also here the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences, many universities, theaters, cinemas, as well as the National Art Gallery, archeological, historical, natural history and other museums. In many places in the city center are kept visible archeological monuments from Roman times


In Sofia you can find many sights such as


Church-monument of St. Alexander Nevsky

The monument was built in honor of the Russian Emperor Alexander II, also called "Tsar Osvoboditel", whose army in 1878 liberated Bulgaria from the five-century Ottoman rule. Alexander Nevsky, after whom the cathedral is named, was a Russian prince (1220-1263) - a great general and diplomat. He is a patron saint of the Russian Emperor Alexander II and is a symbol of Russian military glory.

National Archaeological Museum

Opened in 1892, the Archaeological Museum of Sofia has 5 halls: Central Hall, Prehistory, Middle Ages, Treasure and a special temporary exhibition. It is managed by the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences.

Vitosha mountain

Vitosha is a mountain massif on the outskirts of Sofia. Vitosha is one of the symbols of Sofia and the nearest place for tourism, mountaineering and ski. Convenient bus lines and ropes make the mountain easily accessible.

Church of St. George (Rotunda)

St. George's Church is a late antique red brick rotunda in Sofia, Bulgaria. Built in the early 4th century as a Roman bath, it became a church within the walls of Serdica, the capital of ancient Dacia Mediterranean during the Roman Empire and the Byzantine Empire. The early Christian church is considers the oldest building in modern Sofia and belongs to the Bulgarian Orthodox Church. The building, a cylindrical domed structure built on a square base is known for frescoes from the 12th, 13th and 14th century in the central dome. Three layers of frescoes have been found, the earliest dating from the 10th century. Magnificent frescoes by 22 prophets over 2 meters high crown the dome. Painted during the Ottoman period when the building was used as a mosque, these frescoes were only discovered and restored in the 20th century.

Boris's garden

Borisova Garden is the largest park in Sofia. Its area is 3.02 square kilometers. When it was created, the garden was located on the outskirts of the capital, which with the growth of the city enter its extended center

First the park was called "Razsadnik", later it was called Pipinier. Until 1885 the park was also popular as the Constantinople Garden. After the birth of the heir to the throne Boris Tarnovski was renamed Knyaz-Borisova Garden.

The National Palace of Culture

The National Palace of Culture, located in Sofia, the capital of Bulgaria, is the largest, multifunctional conference and exhibition center in Southeast Europe. It was opened in 1981 honor of the 1300th anniversary of Bulgaria. The center was initiated at the suggestion of Lyudmila Zhivkova, daughter of the communist leader of the former People's Republic of Bulgaria Todor Zhivkov. It was designed by a team from Bulgarian and foreign architects, led by Alexander Georgiev Barov together with Ivan Kanazirev.
