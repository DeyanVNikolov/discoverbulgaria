Bulgarian cities - Lovech


LOVECH WEATHER

Lovech is a town in central northern Bulgaria. During the Ottoman rule it was called "Alton Lovech" - "Golden Lovech". Today Lovech is the administrative and economic center of the eponymous municipality of Lovech and Lovech district. The population of the city according to NSI data as of 31.12.2019 is 31 695 inhabitants.


In Lovech you can find many sights such as


The covered bridge

The covered bridge in Lovech is one of the symbols of the city and one of the most visited local attractions. At the end of the 19th century, the inhabitants of Lovech turned to the self-taught master Kolyu Ficheto with a request to build a covered bridge with shops. He began construction in 1874 and completed it in the winter of 1876. The bridge was 10 m wide and 84 m long. Its foundations were made of stone and the rest of wood.

Ethnographic Museum

The Ethnographic Museum in Lovech is located in two neighboring houses - Drasova and Rashova - in the old quarter "Varosha". The expositions in both houses recreate the home life of the families in Lovech at the end of the XIX and the beginning of the XX century. Built in the first half of the 19th century, Drasova House is one of the most typical houses in the Lovech region, and its architecture is extremely well preserved. The exposition recreates the home system of the wealthy commercial family.

Deli Hamam Old Town Bath

The old town bath of Lovech, built in the XVII-XVIII century, is now converted into the only virtual attraction in Europe dedicated to water. Water museum full of surprises. Inside under a special glass you can see how the ancient hypocaust heating system worked, too so visitors can learn interesting facts about water through specially designed multimedia effects and programs.

Baroque houses

"Baroque houses" is a complex of houses in the western part of the main street "Targovska" on Lovech. They are built in Baroque style. Baroque houses began to be built after the liberation of the city from Ottoman yoke. The first baroque house was built by the Lovech merchant Petar Zlatev. It is distinguished by bay windows, roof towers, arches and capitals.

Zoo

The Lovech Zoo was established in the 1960s and is located in a park "You lose." The zoo is the second largest in Bulgaria. Preferred place to visit by Lovech citizens and guests of the city.

The House of Docho Shipkov

Docho Petrov Shipkov (Mincho) was a participant in the communist guerrilla movement at the time of the Second World War, partisan of the People's Fighting Company "Chavdar" and Partisan Detachment "Hristo Karpachev ". Activist of the Bulgarian Communist Party, politician and MP.
