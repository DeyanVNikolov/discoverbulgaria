Bulgarian cities - Burgas


BURGAS WEATHER

Burgas is the largest city in Southeastern Bulgaria and the second largest the Bulgarian Black Sea coast, and with its land of 253,644 km², it is the second largest city in Bulgaria(after Sofia). According to the last census of GRAO as of September 15, 2021, the population is 210,980 inhabitants and so continues to be the fourth most populous city in the country (after Sofia, Plovdiv and Varna).


In Burgas you can find many sights such as


The Burgas bridge

One of the symbols of the city of Burgas and a favorite place for walks of citizens and guests of the city is the Burgas bridge. Entered nearby 300 meters into the sea, the bridge in Burgas, attracts tourists from near and far. The romance of the bridge enchants not only with the unique view of the endless sea, but and keeps the memory of numerous eyes in love and first kisses. From the 22-meter observatory is revealed amazing views of the mystical Island of St. Anastasia.

Sea station - Burgas

Burgas Sea Station is designed to service cruise ships. From her official opening at the end of 2013 until the end of 2016, 41 liners of 12 cruise companies were accepted and serviced. 127,000 passengers were served, half of whom sailed with the attraction boats, visiting the island of St. Anastasia.

Alyosha Monument

The monument to the Soviet Army in Burgas depicts a Soviet soldier with a raised to the sky left hand. Its height is about 18 meters. The bronze reliefs recreate scenes from the Second World War war and the reception of Soviet troops in Burgas. It is located on the central Troika Square in Burgas.

Petya Dubarova Monument

Petya Dubarova is considered one of the greatest talents in Bulgarian literature. The monument of the Burgas poet is made of bronze by the sculptor Radostin Damaskov. The sculpture is erected in 2010 in honor of the poet, who remained forever at 18 years old. The three-meter bronze a monument depicts Petya Dubarova in full length facing the sea.

The Compass

The compass of Burgas is called the place that measures with architectural accuracy the ideal city center. The "compass" is located on the main street "Alexandrovska". The place marks zero kilometer of Burgas (elevation 0) and is a bronze sculpture - a stylish decorative plate with embossed images. This special sign is the point at which everything else in the city is measured, even with GPS The compass is the work of Radostin Damaskov, and the designer is architect Vladimir Milkov.

Burgas Lake

Burgas Lake is the kingdom of rare birds in Bulgaria. Here is one of the few places where you can see colonies of nesting nocturnal, white and maned herons. They are also among its inhabitants Dalmatian and pink pelicans, as well as gentle thorny-tailed divers. They spend the night in Atanasovsko lake, and during the day they eat in Burgas. The reservoir is located in the heart of Burgas, between the neighborhoods of Gorno and Dolno Ezerovo.
