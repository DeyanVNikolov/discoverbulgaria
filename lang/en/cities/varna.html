Bulgarian Cities - Varna


VARNA WEATHER

It is the largest city in Northern Bulgaria and on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast. The population of the city according to NSI as of December 31, 2019 is estimated at 336,216 people, which puts Varna in third place place in Bulgaria (after Sofia and Plovdiv).On its territory the Admiralty of the Bulgarian Army is located. Varna is often called the "sea capital" or the "summer capital of Bulgaria" and is an important tourist destination educational center, starting point for the resorts on the North Black Sea coast.


In Varna you can find many sights such as


Marine Station

Varna Sea Station serves both cargo ships and cruise and liner passenger ships ships. In 2006 it served over 31,650 domestic and international passengers, as well as 23 large ones cruise ships with 9200 passengers.
Varna Sea Station has 2 berths for cruise ships and a small marina, managed by the port yacht club "Port Varna". The marina offers small vessel services and is a regular host and organizer of many local regattas.

Palace of Culture and Sports

The Palace of Culture and Sports, slang for Sports Hall, is multifunctional complex located in the center of Varna. It is among the famous buildings in the city, hosts cultural events, sporting events, exhibitions and conferences.

Asparuhov bridge

The Asparuhov Bridge connects the Asparuhovo and Galata neighborhoods with the central part of the city, representing the only connection through the navigable canal connecting Varna Lake with the Black Sea. It is part of the Black Sea Highway and European road E87.

Dolphinarium

The Varna Dolphinarium is located in the northern part of the Sea Garden in the city of Varna. It was opened on August 11, 1984 and has 1200 seats. The dimensions of the pool are 12 by 15 meters and depth 6 meters, and is filled with seawater through a special pump system. The water is extracted from Black sea ​​150 - 160 meters from the shore and a depth of 3 - 6 meters, where it is the cleanest and is processed before reaching the pool. The dolphinarium also has a coffee shop with a transparent wall to aquarium. There are five dolphins in the Varna Dolphinarium - 3 female and 2 male - Kimbo, Dolly and Mika (the most famous) are brought from the distant shores of Guatemala and Cuba, and Joanna, Bimbo and Flipper were born in Varna dolphinarium.

The Dormition of the Mother of God Cathedral

Assumption is the largest church in the city of Varna, Bulgaria, the cathedral of The Dioceses of Varna and Veliko Preslav of the Bulgarian Orthodox Church.
In 1879 the imperial Commissioner at the Head of the Provisional Russian Government in Bulgaria Prince Alexander Dondukov-Korsakov during the visit his in Varna remains disappointed with the condition of the Bulgarian Church of St. Archangel Michael and states that is ready to help build a representative Bulgarian church in the city.
On August 22, 1880 after solemn prayer, served by Metropolitan Simeon in the presence of many Bulgarians and Armenians, Prince Alexander I of Bulgaria laid the foundation stone of the cathedral

The Sea Garden

The Sea Garden is one of the symbols of the city of Varna. She has positioned her beautiful and cool alleys along the entire promenade in the city. Characteristic of the Sea Garden are the beautiful ones maintained garden areas, quiet alleys, beautiful diverse natural vegetation and many cultural monuments located on its territory. The impressive central entrance of the Sea Garden, formed by columns was built in 1939 according to the design of the then chief architect Georgi Popov.
After the Liberation, in 1881 the mayor Mikhail Koloni was the first to raise the issue of creating a city garden (behind the Theater) and a modern seaside park. The activity of Mikhail Coloni and his associates quickly gave results - the garden soon reached 26 decares, the first 130 trees were planted, they were repaired the paths.
