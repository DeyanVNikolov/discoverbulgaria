Bulgarian nature - Pirin



With its highest peak Vihren (2914.3 m), Pirin ranks second in height in Bulgaria after Rila (2925 m).



Vihren Peak

The route starts from Vihren hut. The classic road to Vihren is the "Royal Road". The road is almost all the time uneven and with a big slope. It takes between 3 and 3.5 hours to climb to the top.

Melnik Pyramids

Near the smallest town in Bulgaria - Melnik, are the Melnik Pyramids - one of the most interesting natural landmarks in the country. They are rock formations, which are located on an area of ​​50 square meters. km. They are conditionally divided into three sections - Melnik, Karlanov and Rozhen pyramids.

Todorka Peak

Todorka or Todorin peak is a massive peak in Bulgaria, the highest on Todorin's side ridge and in fact the only peak located on it. With its 2746m it ranks 11th in the mountains. It is granite, but not rocky, although it has very steep slopes. It is a relatively short ridge with three peaks, called Big, Medium and Small Todorka, respectively.

Skoka Waterfall

Skoka Waterfall is one of the largest, most beautiful and most bizarre natural landmarks in the South Pirin Mountains. It is known among the locals as "Skoko", because the turbulent waters of the river, after taming their rapid escape from the Pirin Mountains, on the meadow below Garvanova rock suddenly make a headlong jump and fly with terrible force into a deep abyss twenty meters.
