Plovdiv

Plovdiv is a city of over 8000 years, whose past can be traced from prehistory to our days.

Burgas

Burgas is the largest city in Southeastern Bulgaria and the second largest in Bulgaria Black Sea coast, and with its land of 253,644 km², it is the second largest city in Bulgaria (after Sofia).

Learn more
Ruse

Ruse is the largest Bulgarian city along the Danube and the fifth city in size in the country, after Sofia, Plovdiv, Varna and Burgas. It is located on the northeastern border of the country with Romania.

Learn more
Shumen

Shumen is located in the Shumen field, which is open to the southeast and gradually reduces its height; the western parts of the city lie about 280 - 300 m above sea level, and the extreme eastern ones - at 180 - 200 m.

Learn more
Tryavna

The town is located in the valley extension of the Tryavna River, at the northern foot of Tryavna Mountain, 20 km east of Gabrovo, 226 km from the capital Sofia, 152 km from Plovdiv, 259 km from Varna and 207 km from Bourgas.

Lovech

Lovech is located on the border of the Danube hilly plain and the foothills of Stara Mountain. 158 km to the west is Sofia, 159 km to the south is Plovdiv, 306 km to the northeast is Varna, 300 km km to the southeast is Bourgas

Veliko Tarnovo

The city is the 13th largest in Bulgaria with a population of 68,828 people as of December 31, 2019. and is one of the four cities in Bulgaria with positive natural growth from 2006 to 2014.

Learn more
Nessebar

The city is located 36 km northeast of the regional center of Burgas. It is located at peninsula in the southern part of Nessebar municipality. The municipality occupies the northernmost part of the Black Sea coast of the Burgas region. It covers 14 settlements, of which 3 cities - Nessebar, Saint Vlas and Obzor. It is Nessebar the largest of them is the municipal center.

Learn more
Gabrovo

Gabrovo is a city in central Bulgaria, administrative and economic center of the eponymous municipality of Gabrovo and the district of Gabrovo. It is located along the Yantra River at its northern foot of the Shipka part of Stara Planina. In the immediate vicinity of it, in the Uzana area, is located the geographical center of Bulgaria.

Learn more
Vidin

The city is located on the right bank of the Danube, where the Romanian islands are located Little Calafat and Big Calafat and the Bulgarian Kutovo (or Goliya Island), Big Twin and Little Twin. Vidin is located 61 km from Lom, on 145 km from Vratsa, 211 km from the capital Sofia, 385 km from Plovdiv, 509 km from Varna and 625 km from Burgas.

Learn more
Stara Zagora

The city is located in the Stara Zagora field - the eastern part of the Upper Thracian lowlands - between Sarnena Sredna Gora, the Holy Hills, the Monastery Hills, the Sakar Mountains and Chirpan hills, near the river Bedechka with an average altitude of 196 m. It is located 209 km asphalt road and 250 km by rail from Sofia.

Learn more
Panagyurishte

There are dozens of Thracian mounds in the vicinity of the city. In one of them - the mound "Marble", the burial of a Thracian leader was discovered. Not far from it in 1949 was discovered the world famous today Panagyurishte treasure, dating from the IV-III century BC. It is made of pure gold and weighs 6,164 kg.

Learn more
Silistra

Тhe city is located in northeastern Bulgaria, ie. in South Dobrudja. It is located 433 km from the Bulgarian capital Sofia, 124 km from Ruse, 116 km from Razgrad, 112 km from Shumen, 90 km from Dobrich, 15 km from Calarasi, 148 km from Constanţa and 127 km from the Romanian capital Bucharest. The Pan-European Transport Corridor passes through it.

Learn more
Blagoevgrad

Blagoevgrad is a city in Bulgaria. It is economic, cultural and educational center of Southwestern Bulgaria and administrative center of the district of the same name and Municipality. As of December 15, 2021, the city is the 14th most populous in Bulgaria with 73,463 inhabitants.

Learn more
Samokov

Samokov is a town in southwestern Bulgaria. It is located in the Sofia region, 55 km from the city Sofia and 10 km from the resort and tourist complex Borovets. The city is the largest in the area and is administrative center of the Municipality of Samokov.

Learn more
Pomorie

Pomorie is a town in southeastern Bulgaria. It is located in Burgas region. The city is administrative center of Pomorie municipality. According to the NSI as of December 31, 2020 Pomorie has 13,926 inhabitants, village which is the fourth largest city on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast after Varna, Burgas and Nessebar.
