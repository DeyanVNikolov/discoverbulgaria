Bulgarian Cities



Sofia

With a population of over 1.2 million people, Sofia is the largest Bulgarian city. It has many historical and natural landmarks!

Learn More
Varna

Varna is a port city and seaside resort on the Bulgarian Black Sea coast, close to the coastal resorts of Golden Sands, St. Constantine and Albena. It is famous for the "gold of Varna", a 6000-year-old Thracian treasure

Learn More
Plovdiv

Plovdiv is a city of over 8000 years, whose past can be traced from prehistory to the present day. The first Neolithic settlements within the boundaries of today's city began in the VI millennium BC, and since the Stone-Copper Age, life in the city has not been interrupted, which ranks it among the most ancient permanently inhabited cities in the world.

Learn More
Burgas

Burgas is the largest city in Southeastern Bulgaria and the second largest in Bulgaria Black Sea coast, and with its land of 253,644 km², it is the second largest city in Bulgaria (after Sofia).

Learn More
Ruse

Ruse is the largest Bulgarian city along the Danube and the fifth city along size in the country, after Sofia, Plovdiv, Varna and Burgas. It is located on the northeastern border of the country with Romania.

Learn More
Shumen

Shumen is located in the Shumen field, which is open to the southeast and gradually decreases in height; the western parts of the city lie at about 280 - 300 m above sea level, and the extreme eastern - at 180 - 200 m. The city is located at the foot of the Shumen plateau, which has a very diverse nature. Its ridge rises to 500 m above sea level. Its elevation in relation to the city is about 250 - 300 m.

Learn More
Tryavna

The town is located in the valley extension of the Tryavna River, at the northern foot of the Tryavna Mountain, 20 km east of Gabrovo, 226 km from the capital Sofia, 152 km from Plovdiv, 259 km from Varna and 207 km from Bourgas. Neighborhoods of the town are: Bozhkovtsi, Dimiev Khan, Kisiyska Mera, Radantsi, Svetushka, Stoyanovtsi, Hitrevtsi.

Learn More
Lovech

Lovech is located on the border of the Danube hilly plain and the foothills of Stara Planina. 158 km to the west is Sofia, 159 km to the south is Plovdiv, 306 km to the northeast is Varna, 300 km to the southeast is Burgas, 30 km to the south is Troyan, 30 km to the north is Pleven and 30 km to the north is Pleven. km east is Sevlievo. It is located on both sides of the Osam River, which, coming out of a rocky gorge, makes several curves between the hills "Stratesh", "Hisarya" and "Bash Bunar".

Learn More
Veliko Tarnovo

The city is the 13th largest in Bulgaria with a population of 68,828 people as of December 31, 2019 and is one of the four cities in Bulgaria with positive natural growth from 2006 to 2014. The agglomeration of Veliko Tarnovo - Gorna Oryahovitsa and several smaller settlements has a population of over 130,000 people.

Learn more
Nesebar

The city is located 36 km northeast of the regional center of Burgas. It is located on a peninsula in the southern part of Nessebar municipality. The municipality occupies the northernmost part of the Black Sea coast of Buongs region. It covers 14 settlements, of which 3 cities - Nessebar, Saint Vlas and Obzor. Nessebar is the largest of them and is a municipal center.

Learn more
Gabrovo

Gabrovo is a city in central Bulgaria, the administrative and economic center of the eponymous municipality of Gabrovo and the district of Gabrovo. It is located along the Yantra River at the northern foot of the Shipka part of Stara Planina. In close proximity to it, in the Uzana area, is located the geographical center of Bulgaria.
